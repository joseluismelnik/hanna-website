I've lived in a lot of places, but nothing quite prepared me for the moment I first walked out of my apartment and realized — oh, *this* is home.

We live right next to **Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias**, and honestly? I don't think I'll ever get used to how beautiful it is. Every single day, I walk past those white futuristic structures rising out of the water, and I still stop to look. My husband thinks I'm being dramatic. I think he's forgotten how to be amazed. 😄

## The Complex Itself

If you somehow haven't heard of it — Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias is a massive cultural complex designed by the Valencian architect Santiago Calatrava. It stretches along the old Turia riverbed and includes an IMAX cinema and planetarium (**L'Hemisfèric**), a stunning science museum (**Museu de les Ciències**), the **Palau de les Arts** opera house, and **L'Oceanogràfic** — Europe's largest aquarium.

As an IT person, I especially love the science museum. Yes, I go there for fun. Yes, I drag my husband there on weekends. No, I'm not sorry.

But the real magic isn't inside any of the buildings. It's just *being* there — walking along the reflecting pools in the evening when everything turns golden, or early on a Sunday morning when it's almost empty and the city feels like it's just yours.

## The Park Is Everything

What most people don't realize is that Ciudad de las Artes sits inside **Jardí del Túria** — a 9-kilometer linear park that runs through the entire city along the old Turia riverbed. The river was diverted after devastating floods in 1957, and what could have become a motorway (there were plans!) was instead turned into this incredible green lung running through Valencia's heart.

I run here a few times a week. Or at least, I *intend* to run — sometimes it turns into a walk with a coffee from one of the kiosks. The park has playgrounds, sports courts, open lawns, little bridges, orange trees, and always, always people out living their lives. Families, cyclists, dogs, retirees playing cards. It's the most human place I know in this city.

## Ruzafa — My Neighborhood Within a Neighborhood

A short walk from us is **Ruzafa**, which is basically the coolest neighborhood in Valencia right now and has been for a while. It's got that perfect mix of old Valencian character and a very alive, young, international energy.

My go-to Saturday morning routine: walk to Ruzafa, grab breakfast at a café on Calle Sueca or Calle Cadiz, then wander through the **Mercado de Ruzafa** picking up fruit and cheese and pretending I'm going to cook something impressive. (I usually end up ordering pizza. We're both IT people. We're realistic.)

The neighborhood has incredible restaurants, independent boutiques, and some of the best street art in the city. If you visit Valencia and skip Ruzafa, you've made a mistake.

## The Beach Is Just There

Okay, this still feels slightly unreal to me: **La Malvarrosa** beach is about a 20-minute bike ride from our apartment. In spring and autumn it's genuinely perfect — long, wide, not too crowded, with a promenade lined with restaurants serving fresh seafood.

Summer gets busy, but there's something about an evening swim followed by dinner on the *paseo marítimo* that makes the crowds completely irrelevant. Paella on the beach in the city where paella was *invented* hits differently, I promise.

## Living Here as a Remote IT Worker

Working from home here is a genuinely good life. Fast internet, good coffee culture, a climate where you can have your balcony door open for about eight months of the year, and a city that's big enough to have everything you need but human-scaled enough that you don't feel crushed by it.

On stressful days — deploy failed, client call ran long, brain is fried — I take a 10-minute walk to the park and just sit by the water for a bit. It resets something. I don't fully understand the science of it, but I also don't need to. It works. ☀️

---

If you're thinking about moving to Spain or just visiting Valencia, come to this part of the city. Walk the park in the morning, watch the light hit L'Hemisfèric at sunset, have a horchata at **Horchatería Santa Catalina** in the old town, then come back along the Turia.

You'll understand immediately why I'm not going anywhere. 🧡
