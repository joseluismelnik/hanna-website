![Inside Ubik Café — books everywhere](/images/ubik-cafe-interior.png)

There are coffee shops, and then there are *places*. Ubik Café is a place.

It's tucked into Calle del Literato Azorín in Ruzafa — which, if you think about it, is already exactly the right street name for a bookshop-café. You walk in through a corridor of books before you even reach the main room. Floor-to-ceiling shelves everywhere, worn wooden tables, mismatched chairs, and windows that open directly onto the quiet Ruzafa street outside.

I found it on a rainy Tuesday about six months ago when I desperately needed to be somewhere that wasn't my apartment. I sat down with a coffee and a copy of Ursula Le Guin's *The Dispossessed* — which I'd been meaning to finish for weeks — and didn't leave for three hours.

That tells you everything, really.

## The Books

Ubik is officially a hybrid café-bookshop-cultural space, but in practice it just feels like a big, very well-read living room. The shelves are stacked with new titles — narrative non-fiction, poetry, children's books — but also rows of used books you can actually buy at giveaway prices. I've walked out with three books for five euros more than once.

There's no pressure. Nobody circulates asking if you want another drink. You just… exist there, surrounded by books, and it's lovely. 📚

## The Coffee

Solid, honest coffee. Nothing overwrought or precious about it — just a good flat white or a cortado made by people who know what they're doing. The food is Italian-Spanish crossover: focaccia, bruschetta, a daily lunch menu (around €9.50) made with market and organic produce. I've had the salad. It was the kind of salad that makes you feel like you have your life together.

## The Vibe

The reviews call it "inimitable soul" and for once that's not marketing language — it genuinely has that quality of a place that's been itself for so long it couldn't change even if it tried. Everyone seems to know everyone. The furniture is imperfect and worn in exactly the right way. There's WiFi, but nobody seems particularly in a hurry to use it.

![Flat white and a book — the Ubik afternoon](/images/ubik-cafe-coffee.png)

I've brought Andrei here exactly once. He lasted forty minutes before he got restless and went to buy croissants from the place around the corner. I stayed another two hours. This is fine. We are different people. 😄

## When to Go

Ubik opens at 11am Wednesday through Sunday. On Monday and Tuesday mornings it's closed — evenings only. They also host a monthly reading club, book presentations, language exchanges, and occasional live music. If you want to actually meet book-loving locals rather than just sit near them, that's your entry point.

It's a five-minute walk from us — close enough that I sometimes wander over with no plan at all, just because I want to be somewhere that feels like this.
